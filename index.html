Life outside the work

"I'm drawn to ideas that take a long time to understand. The kind that quietly reshape how you see something you thought was settled."

I notice things slowly: how a room feels when people leave it, the exact way someone phrased something. I think in systems but feel in specifics.

I'm an INFJ in the most practical sense. I process a lot internally, care deeply about what and who I give attention to, and tend to connect things across domains before I can explain why.

I've been doing pottery for a little over a year. Every piece carries the decisions you made along the way: a wobble from throwing, a mark from trimming, a glaze that broke differently than expected. You learn to slow down and notice what's actually there, not what you want to see. And to accept that. A piece with a flaw is still a piece.
